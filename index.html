The Six
Tap to expand
01
Bodyweight Squat
15 reps · 2 sec down · 1 sec up
The big one. Direct quad and glute loading. Builds the muscular endurance that failed at km 32.
Form
  • Feet shoulder-width, toes slightly out
  • Sit back like reaching for a chair
  • Knees track over toes — never cave inward
  • Chest up, eyes forward
  • Thighs parallel to floor or below
02
Reverse Lunge
10 reps each leg · alternate
Single-leg matters. Running is single-leg by nature. Bilateral squats alone won't fix the durability gap.
Form
  • Step backward into a long stride
  • Drop back knee toward floor (don't slam it)
  • Push through front heel to return
  • Keep front knee over front foot
  • Torso upright, ribs over hips
03
Single-Leg Glute Bridge
12 reps each leg · squeeze at top
Glutes save quads. Weak glutes mean quads do extra work and fail first. This is the corrective.
Form
  • Lie on back, one knee bent, foot flat
  • Other leg extended straight up
  • Drive through planted heel
  • Lift hips until body forms a line
  • Squeeze glute hard at the top, hold 1 sec
04
Push-Up
10–15 reps · knees if needed
Form insurance. Marathon arm carriage at km 35+ degrades when shoulders and upper back are weak.
Form
  • Hands slightly wider than shoulders
  • Body in straight line — no sagging hips
  • Lower until chest near floor
  • Elbows at ~45° from body, not flared
  • Drop to knees if form breaks
05
Forward Plank
45 seconds · brace hard
Pelvic stability. A wobbling pelvis at km 30 wastes energy and overloads the quads — exactly your wall mechanism.
Form
  • Forearms on ground, elbows under shoulders
  • Body in straight line, head to heels
  • Squeeze glutes, brace abs as if punched
  • No sagging hips, no piked butt
  • Neutral neck — look at floor between hands
06
Side Plank
30 sec each side · hips up
Glute medius. The most underdone area for time-poor runners. Prevents knee cave and is a major injury-prevention exercise.
Form
  • On one forearm, elbow under shoulder
  • Body straight, hips lifted off floor
  • Stack feet (advanced) or stagger them (easier)
  • Don't let bottom hip drop
  • Top arm stretched up or hand on hip

Session Rules

01 Rest 60–90 sec between rounds. Move briskly within a round.
02 Hard floor: 2× per week, every week. Travel weeks too.
03 Form > reps. Stop a set if technique breaks.
04 Best paired with: Tue + Fri. Never the day before a long run.

Progression Ladder

When 3 rounds feels easy, climb the ladder in this order
01
Add a 4th round before adding reps
02
Add reps: squat → 20, lunge → 12/leg, push-up → 18
03
Push-ups: progress to decline (feet on chair) before more reps
04
Squats: progress toward pistol squats (single-leg). This is where real marathon-specific durability is built.
05
Planks: extend to 60 sec / 45 sec side. Then add weight (backpack).
